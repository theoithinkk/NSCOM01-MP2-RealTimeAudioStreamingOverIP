1. PROJECT SPECIFICATIONS & COMPLIANCE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This client fully complies with the project requirements and bonus objectives:

* [REQUIREMENT] SIP SIGNALING: Implements a strict RFC 3261 handshake 
  (INVITE -> 200 OK -> ACK) and session teardown (BYE -> 200 OK). Tracks 
  Call-ID and Tags to maintain dialog state.
* [REQUIREMENT] SDP NEGOTIATION: Fully dynamic. Parses the SIP body to 
  extract the peer's requested RTP/RTCP ports (m=audio) instead of relying 
  on hardcoded destinations.
* [REQUIREMENT] RTP MEDIA: Encapsulates 16-bit PCM audio (L16) into 12-byte 
  binary headers with accurate sequence numbers and timestamps.
* [REQUIREMENT] RTCP STATISTICS: Sends binary Sender Reports (PT 200) every 
  5 seconds. SSRC correctly matches the RTP audio stream.
* [BARE MINIMUM] FILE STREAMING: Transmits 'sample_audio.wav' over UDP.
* [BONUS 1] ONE-WAY MIC: Simplex communication where the caller's mic is ON 
  and the receiver's mic is strictly locked OFF.
* [BONUS 2] TWO-WAY (FULL DUPLEX): Concurrent threading allows both peers to 
  talk and listen simultaneously without walkie-talkie delay.
* [EXTRA] STATE MANAGEMENT: Features manual call answering, loopable 
  ringing/ringback tones, and a 15-second automatic timeout for missed calls.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. HOW TO RUN 
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Open two separate terminal windows.

TERMINAL 1 (The Receiver):
> javac VoIPClient.java
> java VoIPClient
Select Profile '2' (Listens on Ports 5062 / 8002).

TERMINAL 2 (The Caller):
> java VoIPClient
Select Profile '1' (Listens on Ports 5060 / 8000).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. STEP-BY-STEP TESTING & COMPLIANCE PROOF
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--- TEST A: File Stream & SDP ---
1. In Terminal 2, type: 1
2. PROOF OF BARE MINIMUM: Both terminals ring. Terminal 1 types 'answer'. 
   Terminal 1 hears 'sample_audio.wav' playing through their speakers.
3. PROOF OF SDP: In Wireshark (Filter: sip || rtp), inspect the 200 OK 
   packet. Expand the SDP body. Verify "m=audio 8002" successfully 
   told the caller where to route the audio.

--- TEST B: BONUS 1 (ONE-WAY MIC) & MANUAL ANSWER ---
1. In Terminal 2, type: 2
2. Wait 5 seconds to verify the ringing continues.
3. In Terminal 1, type: answer
4. PROOF OF ONE-WAY: The caller speaks into their mic, and the receiver 
   hears it. Check the Receiver's console to verify it says: 
   "[DEBUG] Receiver Mode: LISTENING ONLY. Mic is OFF."

--- TEST C: BONUS 2 (FULL DUPLEX) & RTCP STATS ---
1. Put on headphones. 
2. In Terminal 2, type: 3. In Terminal 1, type: answer.
3. PROOF OF TWO-WAY: Speak into the mic; you will hear your voice echoed 
   back with network delay. In Wireshark, verify RTP packets are flowing 
   simultaneously from Port 8000 -> 8002 AND Port 8002 -> 8000.
4. PROOF OF RTCP: Let the call run for 10 seconds. In Wireshark, look for 
   RTCP Sender Reports (PT=200). Verify the SSRC matches the RTP stream 
   and the Packet Count increases.

--- TEST D: TIMEOUT & STATE MANAGEMENT ---
1. In Terminal 2, type: 2
2. Do absolutely nothing in Terminal 1.
3. PROOF OF TIMEOUT: After exactly 15 seconds, Terminal 2 will print 
   "[TIMEOUT] 15 seconds elapsed" and automatically send a BYE packet 
   to safely tear down the call. Both terminals will reset.
